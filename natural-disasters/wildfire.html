Wildfires

A wildfire is an uncontrolled fire which happens in wildland areas which includes forests, grasslands, brushlands, or other vegetation.

      Before a Wildfire:

    • Don't accumulate flammable materials near your home.
    • Avoid walking or take a drive through floodwaters as it is contaminant.
    • Use fire-resistant materials ,if you stay near wildfire prone areas for roofing and siding.
    • Don't ignore burn bans and red flag warnings.
    • Switch off your electrical appliances
    • Don't panic

      • During a Wildfire:


    • Know your emergency evacuation routes and exits.
    • Don't enter closed areas or restricted zones.
    • Don't touch or handle flammable materials.
    • Have a fire extinguisher and know how to use it.
    • Always be cautious and check for any damages or hazards in your home.
    • Keep yourself abreast about wildlife hotspots in your area.
    • Have a disaster supply kit which consists of all the basic necessities like food, water, and first aid supplies.
    • Be prepared for power outages and have a backup plan.
    • Stay calm,composed and follow evacuation instructions from the authorities.
    • Keep important documents and valuables in a safe place.
    • Wait for authorities to declare it safe before leaving home.
    • Be cautious of wildlife and sharp objects.
    • Scrutinize for burns at your home.

      • Remember!! Remember, wildfires are a serious threat, and prevention, preparedness, and swift action are crucial to mitigate the devastating impacts.!
