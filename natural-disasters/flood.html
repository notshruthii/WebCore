Floods

A flood is the overflow of water often due to heavy rainfall, storm surges, or snowmelt.

    Before a flood:

  • Know your emergency evacuation routes and exits.
  • Secure heavy furniture and objects to walls.
  • Store flammable liquids and gases outside.
  • Have a fire extinguisher and know how to use it.
  • Practice earthquake drills with your family.

    • During a flood:


  • Relocate from the location immediately if ordered by government officials.
  • Avoid walking or take a drive through floodwaters as it is contaminant.
  • Stay away from power lines as it mau cause serious issues.
  • Start to conserve water when you receive a flood alert and prevent yourself using tap water.
  • Let your electrical appliances be away from water
  • Don't panic

    • After a flood:

  • Wait for authorities to declare it safe before leaving home.
  • Be cautious of wildlife and sharp objects.
  • Scrutinize for leakages to your home.
  • Follow boil water instructions from government officials .
  • Keep yourself abreast about flood hotspots in your area.
  • Have a disaster supply kit which consists of all the basic necessities like food, water, and first aid supplies.
  • Be prepared for power outages and have a backup plan.
  • Stay calm,composed and follow evacuation instructions from the authorities.
  • Keep important documents and valuables in a safe place.

    • Remember!! safety is the top priority during floods!
