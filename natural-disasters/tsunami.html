Tsunamis

A tsunami is a series of ocean waves caused by the displacement of a large volume of water. These waves have extremely long wavelengths and periods, traveling at speeds of up to 500 miles per hour and reaching heights of over 30 meters above sea level. Tsunamis can cause widespread destruction and loss of life when they reach coastal areas, resulting in flooding, damage, displacement, and fatalities.

    Before a tsunami:

  • Know your emergency evacuation routes and exits.
  • Give your careful attention to news channels
  • Participate in regular tsunami drills to ensure preparedness..
  • Check for hotspot areas
  • Have a preplan if you live near hotspots. Ensure your home is built to withstand tsunami waves or take steps to reinforce it.

    • During a tsunami

  • Move quickly to higher ground or designated evacuation zones.
  • Watch for fallen power lines, sharp debris, and contaminated water.
  • Move inland and away from the coastline.
  • Pay attention to guidance from local authorities regarding food, water, and shelter.
  • Don't panic

    • After a tsunami:

  • Wait for authorities to declare it safe before leaving home.
  • Only return home when authorities declare it safe.
  • Take photos of property damage for insurance purposes..
  • Don't underestimate risks
  • Keep yourself abreast about Tsunami hotspots in your area.
  • Have a disaster supply kit which consists of all the basic necessities like food, water, and first aid supplies.
  • Be prepared for power outages and have a backup plan.
  • Stay calm,composed and follow evacuation instructions from the authorities.
  • Encourage community preparedness.

    • Remember!! Backup is the top priority during Tsunamis!
