Landslides

A landslide is the movement of rock, debris, or earth down a slope, often caused by a combination of natural and human factors. Landslides can be sudden or gradual, and they can occur on land or underwater.

    Before a landslide:

  • Know your emergency evacuation routes and exits.
  • Give your careful attention to unusual noise, vibrations, or changes in terrain
  • Avoid constructing your homes on steep or unstable slopes.
  • Check for instability
  • Avoid over loading the slopes through construction or excavation.

    • During a landslide


  • Landslides can move quickly, so don't try to outrun them.
  • Avoid driving through areas with active landslides.
  • Get updated with local news and emergency alerts.
  • Stay away from edges of slopes or cliffs.
  • Don't panic

    • After a lanslide:

  • Wait for authorities to declare it safe before leaving home.
  • Only return home when authorities declare it safe.
  • Scrutinize your homes and check for property damage and take photos for insurance
  • Don't underestimate risks
  • Keep yourself abreast about landslides hotspots in your area.
  • Have a disaster supply kit which consists of all the basic necessities like food, water, and first aid supplies.
  • Be prepared for power outages and have a backup plan.
  • Stay calm,composed and follow evacuation instructions from the authorities.
  • Encourage community preparedness.

    • Remember!! Evacuation is the top priority during Landslides!
